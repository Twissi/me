I built my first website in 1999. It was stuffed with tables and background-images, but I loved it. I created pages for friends or random people from the internet just for fun. After finishing school I began to study Computer Science in Dresden. Everything I learned there was totally different from creating a static website.
For my diploma thesis I moved to Munich to work with researchers from Fraunhofer ESK on ELEPHANT, a tool to generate mobile apps. I wrote my master thesis there and I became fascinated with the mobile industry. I wanted to learn from the experts, so I started working for CELLULAR, an agency known for their mobile solutions.

After 3 years of building great mobile websites, it is now time for new challenges.

Side Projects
Cabaret sign

I created this lightbox for the cabaret stage of the "Drunken and sobriety" camp at Nowhere. It has different animations which makes it flash, glow and change colors. It was programmed with an Arduino using an LED strip. The box is made out of lasercutted wood and acrylic glass.

Posted on 17 July | art + festival
Cyborg costume

This half robot half human mask was created for the Nowhere festival. It is made out of plaster and was painted in metallic colors. I used adhesive foil to create the circuit board look and I added some wires and a red LED eye to make it look more mechanical.

Posted on 17 July | art + festival + costume + robot
Boston Challenge App

I built this app in order to challenge my boyfriend during his stay in Boston, MA. Every day he had to solve a challenge and had to prove it by uploading a picture. The app was built with Ruby on Rails and hosted at Heroku. It is optimized for mobile.

Upcycled jellyfish
